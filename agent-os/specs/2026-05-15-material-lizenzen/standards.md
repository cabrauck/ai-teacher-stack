# Standards: Materiallizenzen und Rechte-Metadaten

Status: Draft

## Relevant Standards

- `material-licensing`: source categories, rights metadata, publisher-material boundaries, teacher-created material defaults, and contributor research rules.
- `curriculum-grounding`: public curriculum references stay separate from generated lesson content and publisher material.
- `document-output`: generated exports remain teacher-reviewable and must eventually render visible license metadata.
- `privacy-boundary`: license metadata must not normalize prohibited student data.
- `security-compliance`: no copyrighted textbook content without usage rights, no legal compliance claims, and release packages stay user-safe.
- `contributor-workflow`: research and roadmap work should be represented as GitHub Issues and reviewed by the maintainer.

## Application to this spec

- The first pass is documentation and specification only.
- Publisher material remains referential metadata only unless explicit rights are documented.
- Teacher-created material defaults to planned `CC-BY-SA-4.0` when the teacher chooses to share or publish it.
- Missing or unknown metadata must stay visible and teacher-reviewable.
- Contributor research must separate confirmed facts from open questions.
